01 / About NPCIL

What is NPCIL?

The Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited — a Government of India enterprise responsible for design, construction & operation of nuclear power plants.

🏛

Government Enterprise

NPCIL is a Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) under the Department of Atomic Energy (DAE), Government of India. It operates under the Atomic Energy Act, 1962.

🏢

Headquarters

NPCIL's corporate office is located in Mumbai, Maharashtra. It was incorporated on September 17, 1987 as a public limited company.

⚡

Mission

To develop nuclear power technology and produce electricity in the country in a safe, reliable, and economic manner for the nation's sustainable energy future.

🔬

Technology

NPCIL operates Pressurised Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) and Light Water Reactors (LWR). Indigenous 700 MW PHWRs are NPCIL's flagship technology.

🌍

Role in India's Energy

Nuclear power is India's 5th largest electricity source after thermal, hydro, solar and wind. NPCIL aims to contribute to India's 100 GW nuclear target by 2047.

🛡

Regulatory Oversight

All NPCIL plants are regulated by the Atomic Energy Regulatory Board (AERB), which sets and enforces safety standards for all nuclear facilities in India.

02 / History

Timeline of NPCIL

From India's first reactor in 1956 to 25 operational reactors today — a journey of six decades of nuclear excellence.

1948

Atomic Energy Commission

The Atomic Energy Commission of India is established. Dr. Homi J. Bhabha becomes its first chairman, laying the foundation for India's nuclear programme.

1954

Department of Atomic Energy Founded

DAE is set up directly under the Prime Minister's office. Bhabha Atomic Research Centre (BARC) is established at Trombay, Mumbai.

1963

Tarapur Plant Begins

Construction of India's first nuclear power plant, Tarapur Atomic Power Station (TAPS) in Maharashtra, begins with American Boiling Water Reactor (BWR) technology.

1969

First Nuclear Power — Tarapur

TAPS Unit 1 becomes India's first commissioned nuclear power plant. India officially enters the era of nuclear electricity generation.

1973

RAPP-1 Commissioned

Rajasthan Atomic Power Station Unit 1 begins operation in Rawatbhata — India's first CANDU-type Pressurised Heavy Water Reactor, built with Canadian collaboration.

1987

🎯 NPCIL is Born

Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited is formally incorporated on September 17, 1987 as a government company, separating nuclear power operations from BARC.

2000

MAPS & NAPS Expansion

Madras Atomic Power Station (MAPS) in Tamil Nadu and Narora Atomic Power Station (NAPS) in UP continue steady expansion. India reaches 2,720 MW nuclear capacity.

2013

Kudankulam Begins

Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant Unit 1 is commissioned in Tamil Nadu. Built with Russian VVER-1000 technology, it becomes India's single-largest reactor unit (1,000 MW).

2021

Indigenous 700 MW PHWR Era

Kakrapar Unit 3 (700 MW) becomes India's first fully indigenous 700 MW PHWR to achieve grid connection — a major milestone for India's self-reliant nuclear programme.

2025

RAPP-7 Grid Connection

RAPP Unit 7 (700 MW) at Rawatbhata connects to the Northern Grid. NPCIL now operates 25 reactors with 8,880 MW total capacity. RAPP-8 expected operational in 2025–26.

05 / Exams & Selection

Entrance Exams & Selection Process

NPCIL uses different selection methods depending on the post. Here's a clear breakdown of each pathway.

🏆

GATE — For Executive Trainees

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering

The most prestigious entry into NPCIL. Candidates with valid GATE scores in Mechanical, Chemical, Electrical, Electronics, Instrumentation or Civil engineering are shortlisted.

1Appear in GATE exam (conducted by IITs/IISc)
2Apply on npcilcareers.co.in when notification is released
3Shortlisted in 1:12 ratio based on GATE score (merit list)
4Personal Interview at designated NPCIL centres
5Medical examination & document verification
61-year training → Appointed as Scientific Officer/C
Note: Valid GATE scores from the last 3 years are accepted. GATE 2023 or earlier not accepted for 2026 recruitment. Equal weightage given to scores from all 3 valid years.
📝

Written Test — Trainees & Assistants

For ST/TN, Scientific Assistant, Assistant Grade I

For Stipendiary Trainees (Cat I & II), Scientific Assistants, and Administrative Assistants. NPCIL conducts its own written examination at various centres.

1Notification released on npcilcareers.co.in
2Online application with fee payment (₹100 for Gen/OBC/EWS)
3Written exam: General Aptitude + Technical / Subject Knowledge
4Merit-based shortlisting for interview / document verification
5Medical examination
6Training period (1–3 years) at designated NPCIL station
Exam pattern: Objective MCQ format. Covers English, Maths, General Science, and technical subject matter relevant to the post applied for.

📚 GATE Disciplines Accepted

  • Mechanical Engineering (ME)
  • Chemical Engineering (CH)
  • Electrical Engineering (EE)
  • Electronics & Communication (EC)
  • Instrumentation Engineering (IN)
  • Civil Engineering (CE)

📋 Written Test Topics (Cat-II)

  • General English & Comprehension
  • Numerical Ability & Reasoning
  • Physics (for Operator posts)
  • Chemistry basics
  • Trade-specific technical knowledge (for Maintainer)
  • General Awareness

🩺 Medical Standards

  • Vision: 6/6 (corrected) both eyes
  • No colour blindness
  • Physically fit for industrial work
  • No history of epilepsy or major ailments
  • Medical exam at NPCIL hospital
  • Standards vary by post category

🎯 Age Relaxation

  • SC/ST: 5 years additional
  • OBC (NCL): 3 years additional
  • PwBD (General): 10 years additional
  • PwBD (OBC): 13 years additional
  • PwBD (SC/ST): 15 years additional
  • Ex-Servicemen: as per Govt rules
06 / How to Apply

Step-by-Step Application Guide

A complete walkthrough for a first-time NPCIL applicant — from finding the notification to joining the organisation.

01

Watch for Official Notifications

All NPCIL jobs are advertised on the official recruitment portal npcilcareers.co.in and the main website npcil.nic.in. Do not trust third-party job sites as the sole source. Notifications are also published in major newspapers (Employment News, Times of India, Hindustan).

💡 Bookmark npcilcareers.co.in and check weekly.
02

Read the Notification Carefully

Before applying, read the complete notification PDF. Check eligibility (education, age, discipline), number of vacancies, reservation details, exam pattern, and important dates. Applying without reading can lead to rejection.

💡 Save the notification PDF for future reference during the process.
03

Register & Fill the Online Form

Go to npcilcareers.co.in → Click on the active recruitment link → Register with your email and mobile → Fill in personal, educational, and work details carefully. Upload scanned documents: photo (passport size), signature, caste certificate, education certificates.

💡 Photo: 20–50 KB, JPG. Signature: 10–20 KB, JPG. Keep scans ready before starting.
04

Pay the Application Fee

Fee: ₹100 for General, EWS, OBC candidates (for Trainee posts). ₹500 for Executive Trainee via GATE. Payment is online via Net Banking / Debit Card / Credit Card / UPI. SC, ST, PwBD, Female candidates and NPCIL employees are exempt from fee.

💡 Keep your payment receipt/transaction ID safely. You may need it if any discrepancy arises.
05

Appear for the Exam / GATE

Download your Admit Card from the portal 1–2 weeks before the exam. Carry a valid photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID) to the exam centre. For GATE, your score card from IIT serves as the credential — no separate exam by NPCIL.

💡 Check exam city and centre well in advance. Arrive 30 minutes early.
06

Interview & Medical

Shortlisted candidates are called for a personal interview (for ET via GATE) or direct document verification (for Trainees). After selection, a medical examination is conducted at the NPCIL site hospital. Fitness for nuclear plant work is assessed.

💡 Get a full eye check (including colour vision) and general health check before the medical date.
07

Training & Joining

Selected candidates undergo a mandatory training period (1 year for ET, 1–3 years for Trainees) at NPCIL's Training Centres (NTTC at Mumbai, or site-specific centres). Training covers nuclear technology, reactor safety, plant systems, and on-the-job learning. On successful completion, you are regularised as a permanent NPCIL employee.

💡 Stipend is paid during training. Accommodation is typically provided at the plant township.
